ACME CORPORATION EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Policy Version: 3.2

OVERVIEW
This policy outlines the procedures and guidelines for employee expense reimbursement at ACME Corporation.

ELIGIBLE EXPENSES

1. Travel Expenses
   - Airfare: Economy class only for domestic flights. Business class permitted for international flights exceeding 6 hours.
   - Hotel: Maximum rate of $200 per night for domestic travel, $300 per night for international travel.
   - Ground Transportation: Taxis, ride-sharing services, and rental cars are reimbursable.
   - Mileage: Personal vehicle use reimbursed at $0.67 per mile (2025 IRS rate).

2. Meals and Entertainment
   - Daily meal allowance: $75 per day for domestic travel, $100 per day for international travel.
   - Client entertainment: Maximum $150 per person with manager pre-approval required.
   - Alcohol: Limited to $50 per occasion and must include business justification.

3. Office Supplies and Equipment
   - Office supplies under $100: No pre-approval required.
   - Equipment purchases $100-$500: Manager approval required.
   - Equipment purchases over $500: VP approval required.

SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
- All expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date.
- Receipts are required for all expenses over $25.
- Credit card statements alone are not acceptable documentation.
- Foreign currency expenses must include exchange rate documentation.

REIMBURSEMENT TIMELINE
- Approved expenses are reimbursed within 10 business days via direct deposit.
- Expenses submitted without proper documentation will be rejected and returned to the employee.

NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
- Personal entertainment or leisure activities
- Fines and traffic violations
- Membership fees (gym, clubs, etc.)
- Commuting expenses to primary work location
- Expenses incurred by family members unless pre-approved

POLICY VIOLATIONS
Employees who submit fraudulent expense claims may face disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

CONTACT
