Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Policy Version: 5.0

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Data Privacy and Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting personal data, confidential information, and customer data at ACME Corporation. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, partners, and third-party service providers who access, process, or store data on behalf of ACME or its customers.

ACME is committed to maintaining the highest standards of data privacy and security in compliance with applicable regulations including GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, SOC 2, ISO 27001, and industry-specific requirements.

2. DEFINITIONS

Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, including names, email addresses, IP addresses, location data, and online identifiers.

Sensitive Personal Data: Special categories of personal data requiring enhanced protection, including health information, financial data, biometric data, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, and data concerning sex life or sexual orientation.

Customer Data: All data uploaded, processed, or generated by customers using ACME's platform, including business data, analytics, and derived insights.

Confidential Information: Proprietary information belonging to ACME or customers that is not publicly available, including trade secrets, business strategies, source code, and customer lists.

Data Controller: The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.

Data Processor: The entity that processes personal data on behalf of a data controller.

Data Subject: An individual whose personal data is being processed.

3. DATA PRIVACY PRINCIPLES

ACME adheres to the following core principles:

Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency:
- Process personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently
- Provide clear privacy notices explaining data collection and use
- Obtain appropriate consent when required
- Honor data subject rights promptly

Purpose Limitation:
- Collect data only for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes
- Do not process data in ways incompatible with original purposes
- Document purposes for each data processing activity
- Regularly review data uses for purpose alignment

Data Minimization:
- Collect only data that is adequate, relevant, and necessary
- Avoid collecting excessive or speculative data
- Implement technical controls to enforce minimal collection
- Regularly purge unnecessary data

Accuracy:
- Maintain accurate and up-to-date personal data
- Provide mechanisms for data subjects to update information
- Implement validation controls for critical data fields
- Correct inaccuracies promptly when identified

Storage Limitation:
- Retain personal data only as long as necessary
- Implement automated retention and deletion policies
- Document retention periods for each data category
- Securely dispose of data when retention period expires

Integrity and Confidentiality:
- Protect data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Encrypt sensitive data in transit and at rest
- Maintain audit logs of data access and modifications

Accountability:
- Demonstrate compliance with privacy principles
- Maintain comprehensive documentation of data processing activities
- Conduct regular privacy assessments and audits
- Designate responsible individuals for privacy compliance

5. DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING

ACME classifies all data into four categories with corresponding handling requirements:

PUBLIC DATA (Level 1):
Definition: Information approved for public disclosure
Examples: Marketing materials, press releases, public documentation
Handling Requirements:
- No special restrictions on access or distribution
- May be shared externally without authorization
- Version control for accuracy
Encryption: Not required

INTERNAL DATA (Level 2):
Definition: Information for internal use only, limited external distribution
Examples: Internal policies, organizational charts, project plans
Handling Requirements:
- Accessible to all employees with business need
- Sharing outside ACME requires manager approval
- Must not be posted on public websites or forums
Encryption: Required for external transmission

CONFIDENTIAL DATA (Level 3):
Definition: Sensitive business information requiring protection
Examples: Financial data, strategic plans, employee personal information, customer business data
Handling Requirements:
- Access restricted to authorized personnel only
- Multi-factor authentication required for access
- Sharing requires data classification labels
- Must be encrypted in transit and at rest
- Watermarked when printed
- Secure deletion when no longer needed
- Cannot be stored on personal devices without approval
Encryption: Required (AES-256 or equivalent)

RESTRICTED DATA (Level 4):
Definition: Highly sensitive data subject to regulatory requirements
Examples: Payment card data, protected health information, social security numbers, authentication credentials
Handling Requirements:
- Strictly limited access on need-to-know basis
- Enhanced authentication (privileged access management)
- All access logged and monitored
- Masked in non-production environments
- Cannot be downloaded or printed without justification
- Encrypted with strongest available algorithms
- Separate network segmentation where applicable
- Annual access recertification required
Encryption: Required (AES-256 minimum, hardware security modules for keys)

6. CUSTOMER DATA PROTECTION

Role as Data Processor:
For customer data processed through our platform, ACME acts as a data processor. Customers remain data controllers responsible for lawfulness of processing.

Customer Data Commitments:

Data Ownership:
- Customers retain all rights to their data
- ACME claims no ownership of customer data
- Customers may export all data at any time
- ACME will not use customer data for purposes other than providing services

Data Isolation:
- Logical separation of customer data using tenant identifiers
- No commingling of data between customers
- Dedicated encryption keys per customer environment
- Role-based access controls enforce isolation

Data Location and Residency:
- Customers choose geographic region for data storage
- Data remains in chosen region unless customer authorizes transfer
- Available regions: US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, Singapore, Brazil
- Sub-processors documented and approved by customer

Data Processing Agreement (DPA):
- DPA executed with all enterprise customers
- Specifies purpose, duration, and nature of processing
- Documents security measures and sub-processors
- Addresses data subject rights fulfillment
- Includes Standard Contractual Clauses for EU transfers

Subprocessor Management:
- Maintain current list of all subprocessors
- Notify customers 30 days before adding new subprocessors
- Customers may object to new subprocessors
- Ensure subprocessors meet same data protection standards
- Flow down data protection obligations contractually

7. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

ACME respects and facilitates the following data subject rights:

Right to Be Informed:
- Provide clear privacy notices at data collection
- Explain purposes, legal bases, retention periods
- Identify data controller and DPO contact information
- Privacy notice available in multiple languages

Right of Access:
- Data subjects may request copy of their personal data
- Response within 30 days (45 days for complex requests)
- Provide data in structured, machine-readable format
- No fee for initial request (reasonable fee for excessive requests)
- Identity verification required to prevent unauthorized disclosure

Right to Rectification:
- Data subjects may request correction of inaccurate data
- Correction completed within 30 days
- Notify third parties of corrections where feasible
- Self-service correction options in user interfaces

Right to Erasure ("Right to Be Forgotten"):
- Data subjects may request deletion of personal data
- Applies when data no longer necessary, consent withdrawn, or unlawful processing
- Exceptions for legal compliance, legal claims, public interest
- Complete deletion within 30 days when applicable
- Notify subprocessors to delete as well

Right to Restrict Processing:
- Data subjects may request limitation on processing
- Applied during dispute over accuracy or lawfulness
- Data retained but not actively processed
- Notification before restriction is lifted

Right to Data Portability:
- Provide personal data in structured, commonly used format
- Allow transmission directly to another controller where feasible
- Applies to data provided by data subject and processed by automated means

Right to Object:
- Data subjects may object to processing for direct marketing (absolute right)
- May object to processing based on legitimate interests (rebuttable)
- ACME must cease processing unless compelling legitimate grounds

Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making:
- Inform data subjects of automated decision-making and profiling
- Provide opportunity to obtain human intervention
- Allow data subject to express view and contest decision
- Regular review of automated decision logic and fairness

Request Handling Process:
1. Requests submitted via privacy@acme.corp or privacy web form
2. Identity verification using two-factor authentication
3. Request logged in privacy request management system
4. Assignment to appropriate team for fulfillment
5. Response within regulatory timeframes
6. Documentation maintained for audit purposes

8. SECURITY CONTROLS

Administrative Controls:

Security Policies and Procedures:
- Comprehensive information security program documented
- Policies reviewed and updated annually
- Procedures for all critical security processes
- Exception process for policy deviations

Risk Assessments:
- Annual enterprise risk assessment
- Privacy impact assessments for new products and features
- Vendor risk assessments before engagement
- Continuous monitoring of threat landscape

Security Training:
- Mandatory annual security awareness training for all employees
- Role-specific training for engineers, support staff
- Phishing simulation exercises quarterly

- Specialized training for privileged users

Incident Response:
- 24/7 security operations center monitoring
- Documented incident response plan tested quarterly
- Classification and escalation procedures
- Customer notification within 72 hours for material incidents
- Regulatory notification as required by applicable laws
- Post-incident review and lessons learned

Technical Controls:

Access Control:
- Unique user accounts for all personnel
- Multi-factor authentication for all system access
- Privileged access management for administrative functions
- Role-based access control with principle of least privilege
- Annual access reviews and recertification
- Immediate revocation upon termination

Network Security:
- Firewalls at network perimeters
- Network segmentation isolating sensitive systems
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems
- Web application firewalls for all public-facing applications
- DDoS protection services
- Virtual private networks for remote access

Encryption:
- TLS 1.3 for all data in transit
- AES-256 encryption for data at rest
- Encrypted database backups
- Hardware security modules for key management
- Key rotation policies and procedures
- Full disk encryption on all laptops and mobile devices

Vulnerability Management:
- Weekly automated vulnerability scans
- Quarterly penetration testing by third-party
- Patch management with 30-day SLA for critical patches
- Security testing integrated into development pipeline
- Bug bounty program for responsible disclosure

Logging and Monitoring:
- Centralized logging for all systems
- Real-time security event monitoring
- Log retention for 90 days online, 7 years archived
- Automated alerts for suspicious activity
- Security information and event management (SIEM) platform

Data Loss Prevention:
- DLP software monitoring for sensitive data exfiltration
- Email filtering and attachment scanning
- USB port controls on corporate devices
- Cloud access security broker (CASB) for SaaS applications
- Watermarking on confidential documents

Physical Controls:

Data Center Security:
- ISO 27001 certified data centers
- 24/7 physical security staff
- Biometric access controls
- Video surveillance with 90-day retention
- Visitor log and escort requirements
- Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control)
- Redundant power and network connectivity

Office Security:
- Badge access to all office locations
- Visitor sign-in and escort policy
- Security desk at main facilities
- Locked areas for confidential materials
- Clean desk policy enforcement
- Secure disposal bins for confidential documents

Device Security:
- Corporate-issued devices for access to sensitive data
- Full disk encryption on all devices
- Remote wipe capabilities
- Lost/stolen device reporting procedures
- Secure device disposal and recycling

9. DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL

General Retention Principles:
- Retain data only as long as necessary for legitimate business purposes
- Consider legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements
- Balance retention needs with privacy principles
- Document retention periods for all data categories

Retention Schedules:

Customer Data:
- Active subscription period plus 30 days
- Extended upon customer request for up to 90 days
- Deleted automatically after retention period expires
- Customer may request deletion at any time
- Backups retained for 90 days then overwritten

Employee Records:
- Active employment plus 7 years
- Covers personnel files, compensation, performance reviews
- Subset of data retained longer for specific legal requirements
- Former employee data access requests honored

Financial Records:
- 7 years from end of fiscal year
- Complies with tax and accounting regulations
- Includes invoices, receipts, bank statements, financial reports

Marketing and Sales Data:
- Opt-out requests honored immediately
- Inactive prospects purged after 3 years
- Customer relationship data retained per customer retention schedule

Application Logs:
- 90 days online retention
- 7 years archived for security and compliance logs
- Automated log rotation and purging

Backup Data:
- Daily backups retained 90 days
- Monthly backups retained 1 year
- Annual backups retained 7 years
- Deleted data may persist in backups until backup rotation

Secure Disposal Procedures:

Electronic Data:
- Cryptographic erasure (destroy encryption keys)
- Overwriting with random data (DoD 5220.22-M standard)
- Physical destruction of storage media when decommissioned
- Certificate of destruction for sensitive media
- Degaussing for magnetic media

Physical Documents:
- Cross-cut shredding for confidential documents
- Locked bins for documents awaiting destruction
- Chain of custody for destruction process
- Certificates of destruction maintained

Devices:
- Data wiping tools certified for complete erasure
- Physical destruction for devices with non-removable storage
- Vendor disposal policies for leased equipment
- Asset tracking through disposal process

12. BREACH NOTIFICATION

Definition of Personal Data Breach:
A breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data.

Breach Response Process:

Detection and Containment (0-2 hours):
- Identify nature and scope of breach
- Contain breach to prevent further exposure
- Preserve evidence for investigation
- Activate incident response team

Assessment (2-24 hours):
- Determine categories and approximate number of affected individuals
- Assess likely consequences and severity
- Identify root cause
- Document timeline of events

Notification Preparation (24-72 hours):
- Draft notification content
- Identify notification recipients (regulators, customers, individuals)
- Determine notification method and timing
- Prepare FAQ and customer support response

Regulatory Notification:
- Notify lead supervisory authority within 72 hours when required
- Include nature of breach, categories and numbers affected, likely consequences
- Describe measures taken and proposed to address breach
- Provide contact point for further information
- Document reasons if notification delayed beyond 72 hours

Individual Notification:
- Notify affected individuals without undue delay when high risk to rights and freedoms
- Describe nature of breach in clear and plain language
- Provide contact point for further information
- Describe likely consequences of breach
- Recommend measures individuals can take to mitigate risk
- May use public communication if individual notification would be disproportionate effort

Customer Notification:
- Notify affected customers within 72 hours of confirmation
- Provide details sufficient for customer to assess impact
- Describe ACME's remediation steps
- Offer support and resources to affected customers
- Executive-level communication for material breaches

Post-Incident Activities:
- Root cause analysis within 30 days
- Implement corrective actions
- Update policies and procedures as needed
- Conduct training on lessons learned
- Document incident in breach register

13. PRIVACY BY DESIGN AND DEFAULT

Privacy by Design Principles:

Proactive not Reactive:
- Anticipate and prevent privacy risks before they materialize
- Embed privacy considerations at earliest stages of design
- Conduct privacy impact assessments for new products and features

Privacy as Default Setting:
- Maximum privacy protection by default
- Users should not need to take action to protect privacy
- Opt-in rather than opt-out for data collection beyond core service

Privacy Embedded into Design:
- Privacy as core functionality, not add-on
- Full integration of privacy throughout system architecture
- Privacy requirements in technical specifications

Full Functionality (Positive-Sum):
- Avoid false trade-offs between privacy and functionality
- Demonstrate both privacy and business value can coexist
- Innovative solutions that achieve multiple objectives

End-to-End Security:
- Secure data throughout lifecycle from collection to deletion
- Strong encryption, minimal collection, secure disposal
- Continuous monitoring and improvement

Visibility and Transparency:
- Users understand what data is collected and how it's used
- Clear privacy notices and consent mechanisms
- Audit trails for accountability

Respect for User Privacy:
- User-centric design with privacy controls
- Easy-to-use privacy settings
- Honor user choices and preferences

Implementation:

Privacy Impact Assessments:
- Required for all new products, features, and data processing activities
- Conducted before project implementation
- Document data flows, privacy risks, and mitigation measures
- Reviewed and approved by DPO

Privacy Requirements:
- Privacy requirements documented alongside functional requirements
- Privacy acceptance criteria in definition of done
- Privacy testing integrated into QA process
- Privacy considerations in code reviews

Data Minimization:
- Collect only data necessary for specified purpose
- Avoid speculative data collection
- Regularly review data collection practices
- Automated deletion of unnecessary data

User Controls:
- Granular privacy settings and preferences
- Self-service data access, correction, and deletion
- Easy-to-find privacy information
- Consent management for optional data uses
