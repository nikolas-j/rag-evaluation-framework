ACME CORPORATION
REMOTE WORK AND FLEXIBLE WORKPLACE POLICY
Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Policy Version: 3.0
Last Reviewed: December 1, 2025

1. POLICY OVERVIEW

ACME Corporation is committed to providing flexible work arrangements that support productivity, employee well-being, and business objectives. This policy establishes guidelines for remote work, hybrid work arrangements, and flexible scheduling to accommodate diverse employee needs while maintaining operational excellence.

Scope: This policy applies to all ACME employees globally, subject to local labor laws and regulations. Contractors and temporary workers should consult their agencies regarding work arrangements.

2. WORK ARRANGEMENT OPTIONS

ACME offers four primary work arrangement models:

OFFICE-BASED (Traditional):
Description: Employee works primarily from ACME office location
Schedule: Standard office hours (typically 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM local time)
Office Presence: 5 days per week minimum
Who: Roles requiring physical presence (facilities, lab technicians, certain IT roles)
Approval: Default arrangement, no special approval required

HYBRID (Recommended Default):
Description: Employee splits time between office and remote locations
Schedule: Flexible with core collaboration hours
Office Presence: Minimum 2 days per week (typically Tuesday-Thursday for team collaboration)
Remote Days: Maximum 3 days per week
Who: Most employees including engineering, product, sales, marketing, customer success
Approval: Manager approval, must align with team collaboration needs

REMOTE-FIRST:
Description: Employee works primarily from home office or remote location
Schedule: Flexible hours with overlap with team time zones
Office Presence: Quarterly in-person meetings (4 times per year), annual company events
Travel Requirement: Willingness to travel for critical meetings and events
Who: Roles that can be performed effectively without regular office presence
Approval: VP approval required, based on role requirements and performance history
Requirements: Minimum 1 year tenure, demonstrated self-management skills

FULLY REMOTE (Location-Independent):
Description: Employee may work from anywhere, including different cities/countries
Schedule: Must maintain 4-hour overlap with core team hours
Office Presence: Semi-annual in-person meetings, annual company events
Geographic Restrictions: Must work from country where ACME has legal entity or employment infrastructure
Who: Senior individual contributors and roles with proven independent productivity
Approval: SVP approval required, limited to 20% of any team
Requirements: Minimum 2 years tenure, exceptional performance history, role suitability

3. ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

General Eligibility Criteria:

Performance Standards:
- Must meet or exceed performance expectations
- No active performance improvement plans
- Demonstrated ability to work independently
- Strong communication skills
- Reliable track record of meeting deadlines

Technical Requirements:
- Reliable high-speed internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps download, 5 Mbps upload)
- Dedicated workspace conducive to productivity
- Ability to participate in video conferences
- Compliance with IT security requirements
- Availability during core business hours or agreed schedule

Role Suitability Assessment:
Managers evaluate roles based on:
- Nature of work (can it be performed remotely?)
- Collaboration requirements
- Customer interaction needs
- Security and compliance considerations
- Team dynamics and culture
- Equipment and resource requirements

Positions Typically Ineligible for Full Remote:
- Facilities and physical security staff
- Laboratory technicians requiring specialized equipment
- Roles with daily physical deliverable requirements
- New employee onboarding coordinators (first 90 days)
- Positions handling physical confidential materials

Trial Period:
- All remote/hybrid arrangements include 90-day trial period
- Manager evaluates productivity, communication, and collaboration
- Arrangement may be modified or terminated if unsuccessful
- Employee and manager meet at 30, 60, and 90 days to assess

4. APPLICATION AND APPROVAL PROCESS

Step 1: Self-Assessment
Employee considers:
- Can my role be performed effectively with proposed arrangement?
- Do I have appropriate workspace and technology?
- Can I maintain productivity and collaboration?
- How will this impact my team and customers?

Step 2: Proposal Submission
Employee submits request including:
- Desired work arrangement (hybrid, remote-first, fully remote)
- Proposed schedule and office presence commitment
- Rationale and business justification
- Description of remote workspace and technology setup
- Plan for maintaining collaboration and communication

Step 3: Manager Review
Manager evaluates:
- Role suitability for remote work
- Employee performance and work history
- Team coverage and collaboration needs
- Business operational requirements
- Customer impact considerations

Manager discusses with employee within 10 business days

Step 4: Approval Authority
- Hybrid: Manager approval
- Remote-First: VP approval (manager recommends)
- Fully Remote: SVP approval (VP endorses)

Approval factors:
- Role requirements and nature of work
- Employee performance and tenure
- Team distribution and collaboration needs
- Business objectives and customer needs
- Equity across team members

Step 5: Documentation
HR documents approved arrangement including:
- Work arrangement type and effective date
- Schedule expectations and office presence requirements
- Technology and security requirements
- Review and renewal timeline
- Modification and termination conditions

Step 6: Setup and Onboarding
Employee completes:
- IT security and remote work training
- Home office setup (ergonomics guidance provided)
- Test video conferencing and collaboration tools
- Establish communication norms with team
- IT security review of home network

5. WORK SCHEDULE AND EXPECTATIONS

Core Collaboration Hours:
To ensure effective teamwork across time zones, employees must be available during core hours:

Americas Teams:
- Pacific Time: 10:00 AM - 2:00 PM PT
- Eastern Time: 1:00 PM - 5:00 PM ET
- Overlap ensures cross-coast collaboration

EMEA Teams:
- Central European Time: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM CET
- Covers UK, Continental Europe, Middle East

APAC Teams:
- Japan/Singapore Time: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM JST/SGT
- Covers East Asia and Australia markets

Flexible Hours Outside Core Time:
- Employees may adjust start/end times around core hours
- Must work agreed full-time equivalent (typically 40 hours/week)
- Coordinate with manager and team for coverage
- Document schedule in team calendar
- Maintain appropriate work-life boundaries

Schedule Changes:
- Notify team of significant schedule variations
- Update calendar for availability visibility
- Provide advance notice for extended unavailability
- Be flexible for critical meetings outside normal hours

Meeting Expectations:
- Camera-on for team meetings and collaboration sessions
- Camera optional for large all-hands or training sessions
- Join meetings on time from professional environment
- Minimize background distractions
- Use mute when not speaking
- Professional attire (business casual minimum)

Response Time Commitments:
- Email: Respond within 24 hours (4 hours for urgent)
- Slack/Teams: Respond within 4 hours during core hours
- Calendar invites: Accept/decline within 48 hours
- Set status when unavailable (lunch, focused work, etc.)
- Use auto-responders when out of office

6. HYBRID WORK GUIDELINES

Office Days:
- Minimum 2 days per week in office (guideline: Tuesday-Thursday)
- Teams coordinate to ensure collaboration and mentorship
- All-team days designated monthly (usually mid-month Thursday)
- Adjust around company events and quarterly meetings

Strategic Use of Office Time:
Prioritize office days for:
- Team meetings and collaborative working sessions
- One-on-one meetings with manager
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Client meetings when hosted at office
- Learning and development activities
- Networking and culture building

Remote Days:
Optimize remote days for:
- Focused individual work (coding, writing, analysis)
- Deep concentration tasks
- Meetings that don't require physical presence
- International team calls outside office hours
- Reducing commute time for work-life balance

Desk Hoteling:
- ACME uses "hotelling" (unassigned seating) in most offices
- Reserve desk through office management system
- Lockers available for personal item storage
- Clean desk at end of day
- Designated team neighborhoods in office
- Personal assigned desks available for specific roles

7. REMOTE WORK REQUIREMENTS

Home Office Requirements:

Workspace:
- Dedicated space for work (separate from living areas when possible)
- Ergonomic chair and desk at appropriate height
- Good lighting to reduce eye strain
- Professional background for video calls
- Quiet environment minimizing disruptions
- Climate controlled for comfort and equipment

Technology:
- Company-provided laptop and peripherals
- Reliable internet: 25 Mbps download / 5 Mbps upload minimum
- Backup connectivity option (mobile hotspot)
- Webcam (built-in or external) with 720p minimum resolution
- Headset with microphone for clear audio
- Second monitor (provided by ACME if needed)

Security:
- Secure, password-protected home WiFi network
- No shared devices for work purposes
- Encrypted hard drive (automatic on company devices)
- Virtual Private Network (VPN) for sensitive data access
- Screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- No public WiFi for accessing confidential data
- Compliance with ACME security policies

Office Setup Stipend:
ACME provides one-time home office stipend:
- Remote-First employees: $1,000 USD (or local currency equivalent)
- Hybrid employees working remotely 2+ days/week: $500 USD

Eligible expenses:
- Desk and ergonomic chair
- Monitor, keyboard, mouse
- Lighting
- Headset and webcam
- Office supplies

Submit receipts within 90 days of arrangement approval

9. COMMUNICATION AND COLLABORATION

Communication Principles:

Overcommunicate:
- Default to transparency and over-sharing information
- Document decisions and discussions in writing
- Proactive status updates to team and manager
- Make work visible through shared documents and updates

Asynchronous First:
- Prefer written communication over synchronous meetings
- Document meeting outcomes for absent team members
- Record important meetings for reference
- Use collaborative documents for input and feedback

Meeting Best Practices:
- Always include clear agenda in calendar invite
- Circulate pre-read materials 24 hours in advance
- Start and end on time
- Document decisions and action items
- Send meeting notes within 24 hours
- Consider if meeting could be an email

Tools and Channels:

Email:
- Formal communication, external contacts, documentation
- Include clear subject lines
- Keep cc: lists manageable
- Expected response: 24 hours

Slack/Teams:
- Real-time communication and quick questions
- Use channels vs. DMs for transparency
- Thread conversations to keep channels organized
- Set status to indicate availability
- Expected response: 4 hours during core hours

Video Conferencing:
- Team meetings, one-on-ones, collaborative sessions
- Zoom for external meetings
- Teams for internal meetings
- Record when useful for absent participants

Project Management Tools:
- Jira for engineering projects
- Asana for cross-functional initiatives
- Update status at least weekly
- Tag people for actions and decisions

Shared Documents:
- Google Workspace for collaboration
- Single source of truth for project information
- Version control and clear ownership
- Comment features for feedback

10. MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES

Managing Remote and Hybrid Teams:

Clear Expectations:
- Define goals and success metrics clearly
- Document performance expectations
- Set deliverable deadlines
- Clarify communication norms
- Establish response time expectations

Regular Check-ins:
- Weekly one-on-ones minimum (30-60 minutes)
- Daily team standup or async check-in
- Monthly team meetings for alignment
- Quarterly goal review and feedback

Focus on Outcomes:
- Measure results, not hours worked
- Evaluate quality and impact of work
- Avoid micromanagement and surveillance
- Trust employees to manage time effectively

Team Culture:
- Foster inclusion of remote and office workers equally
- Celebrate wins and milestones virtually
- Facilitate team bonding activities
- Address issues and conflicts promptly
- Model healthy work-life boundaries

Professional Development:
- Equal access to training and development
- Career growth opportunities regardless of location
- Mentorship and coaching
- Support conference attendance (virtual and in-person)

Technology Enablement:
- Ensure team has tools needed for success
- Troubleshoot technology issues promptly
- Provide training on new tools and platforms
- Advocate for team needs with IT and leadership

Fairness and Equity:
- No favoritism based on office presence
- Promotion and compensation decisions based on performance
- Equal information access for remote and office workers
- Rotation of less desirable tasks and schedules

11. EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES

Productivity and Performance:
- Maintain or improve productivity in remote arrangement
- Meet deadlines and quality standards
- Communicate proactively about challenges
- Seek feedback and continuous improvement
- Deliver results aligned with team goals

Communication:
- Be responsive during core hours
- Proactively share progress and blockers
- Over-communicate to compensate for physical distance
- Keep calendar updated with availability
- Use video for important discussions

Collaboration:
- Actively participate in team meetings
- Contribute to team culture and morale
- Support teammates and share knowledge
- Be inclusive of all team members
- Build relationships across remote and office colleagues

Boundaries:
- Maintain regular work schedule
- Take breaks and lunch away from desk
- Use vacation time to recharge
- Set clear work hours with household members
- Disconnect after work hours (except during on-call)
- Avoid working while sick

Security and Compliance:
- Follow all IT security policies
- Protect confidential information
- Use VPN for sensitive data access
- Report security incidents immediately
- Maintain secure home office environment
- Complete required security training

Workspace:
- Maintain professional, ergonomic workspace
- Ensure reliable internet connectivity
- Minimize background noise and distractions during meetings
- Professional appearance for video calls
- Respect confidential information privacy

12. INTERNATIONAL AND CROSS-BORDER CONSIDERATIONS

Working from Different Countries:

Temporary International Work (less than 90 days/year):
- Employees may work temporarily from other countries with approval
- Advance notice required (minimum 2 weeks)
- Must not create tax or employment nexus
- Maintain core hour overlap with team
- Limited to countries where ACME has entity or PEO relationship

Permanent International Relocation:
- Requires formal approval from VP and HR
- ACME must have legal entity or PEO in target country
- May require role or compensation adjustment
- Immigration and work authorization employee's responsibility
- Tax implications and treaty benefits review required
- Minimum 90-day notice required

Current ACME Employment Countries:
- United States (all states)
- Canada (all provinces)
- United Kingdom
- Ireland
- Germany
- France
- Netherlands
- Sweden
- Switzerland
- Australia
- Singapore
- Japan
- India
- Brazil

Digital Nomad Arrangements:
- Available for Fully Remote employees only
- SVP approval required
- Maximum 90 days in any single location (unless authorized for longer)
- Must maintain core hour overlap
- Internet connectivity verification required
- Travel insurance and health coverage confirmation
- Tax and social security implications employee responsibility
- Regular check-ins with manager (at least weekly)

Time Zone Expectations:
- Fully Remote employees must maintain 4-hour overlap with core team hours
- May shift schedule to accommodate time zone differences
- Attend critical meetings even if outside normal hours
- Limit time zone difference to +/- 8 hours from team
- Temporary exceptions for family emergencies or special circumstances

Tax and Legal Compliance:
- Employee responsible for understanding tax implications
- Notify HR of location changes >30 days
- ACME may adjust withholding based on work location
- Employee responsible for state/provincial tax filings
- Work with ACME payroll on location-based requirements

13. PARENTAL AND CAREGIVER FLEXIBILITY

ACME recognizes the challenges of balancing work with parenting and caregiving responsibilities.

Flexibility for Parents/Caregivers:
- Adjusted core hours to accommodate school drop-off/pickup
- Ability to take breaks for caregiving needs
- Make up hours outside traditional schedule
- Prioritize outcomes over specific hours worked
- Use sick leave for family care (per company policy)

Return from Parental Leave:
- Option for gradual return (50%, 75%, 100% over first month)
- Flexible schedule during transition period
- Extended remote work option for first 6 months after return
- Support for nursing mothers (private space, breaks)

Emergency Caregiving:
- Flexibility for unexpected school closures
- Accommodation for sick family member care
- Last-minute schedule adjustments when needed
- Backup care resources and support

Children and Dependents During Work Hours:
- Understand that caregiving emergencies happen
- Children may briefly appear on video calls
- Background noise accommodated with understanding
- Not a substitute for regular childcare during work hours
- Professional conduct and focus still expected

15. HEALTH, SAFETY, AND ERGONOMICS

Ergonomic Best Practices:

Monitor Position:
- Top of screen at or slightly below eye level
- 20-24 inches from eyes
- Directly in front of you (not angled)
- Brightness adjusted to match ambient lighting

Chair and Desk:
- Adjustable chair with lumbar support
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Elbows at 90-degree angle when typing
- Keyboard and mouse at same level
- Desk height allowing relaxed shoulders

Lighting:
- Avoid glare on screen
- Sufficient light to reduce eye strain
- Natural light when possible
- Task lighting for documents

Breaks and Movement:
- 20-20-20 rule: Every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds
- Stand and stretch hourly
- Walk during phone calls when possible
- Lunch away from desk
- Regular exercise and movement

Ergonomic Resources:
- ACME provides virtual ergonomic assessments
- Video tutorials on proper setup
- Reimbursement for ergonomic accessories
- Consult with occupational health if experiencing discomfort

Workers' Compensation:
- Work-related injuries covered regardless of location
- Report injuries immediately to manager and HR
- Document circumstances of injury
- Seek medical attention as appropriate
- Follow claim process per policy

Mental Health and Wellbeing:
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) available 24/7
- Mental health days encouraged (use sick time)
- Virtual wellness programs and resources
- Meditation and mindfulness app subscriptions
- Manager check-ins on workload and stress
- Right to disconnect outside work hours

17. MODIFICATION AND TERMINATION

Arrangement Review:
- Manager reviews remote arrangement performance at 90 days, 6 months, annually
- Employee and manager discuss what's working and challenges
- Adjustments made as needed
- Renewal decision based on performance and business needs

Modification of Arrangement:
May be modified due to:
- Changes in role responsibilities
- Team structure changes
- Business operational needs
- Performance concerns
- Technology or security issues
- Personal circumstances changes

Notice for Modifications:
- ACME will provide 30 days notice when possible
- Employee provides 2 weeks notice for requested changes
- Immediate changes may be required for security or compliance

Termination of Arrangement:
Arrangement may be terminated for:
- Unsatisfactory performance
- Violation of remote work policies
- Security or compliance breaches
- Business necessity requiring office presence
- Role change incompatible with remote work

Transition Upon Termination:
- Return to office-based arrangement (if employment continues)
- 30-day transition period when possible
- Equipment return or reassignment
- Expense adjustment (internet, phone stipends)

Return of Equipment:
Upon employment termination or arrangement change:
- Return all company equipment within 10 days
- Coordinate with IT for pickup or shipping
- Complete offboarding checklist
- Confirm data deletion from personal devices
- Final expense reimbursement submitted
